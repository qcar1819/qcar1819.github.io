Utah Hummingbird Species

  1. Black-Chinned Hummingbird
    • Appearance: Males have a black throat with a purple band; females are green with white underparts.
    • Habitat: Common in canyons, rivers, and urban areas.
    • Behavior: Territorial, often perches on bare branches.
  2. Broad-Tailed Hummingbird
    • Appearance: Males have a rose-magenta throat; females have green spots on throat.
    • Habitat: Breeds in high mountain meadows up to 10,500 feet.
    • Behavior: Males perform diving displays with a trilling sound.
  3. Calliope Hummingbird
    • Appearance: Smallest U.S. bird; males have magenta throat streaks.
    • Habitat: Found in high-elevation forests and meadows.
    • Behavior: Migrates 5,000 miles to Mexico.
  4. Rufous Hummingbird
    • Appearance: Males are bright orange; females have rusty flanks.
    • Habitat: Passes through Utah during migration.
    • Behavior: Highly aggressive, chases other birds.
  5. Costa's Hummingbird
    • Appearance: Males have a purple throat and forehead; females are green and white.
    • Habitat: Rare in Utah, seen in southwestern deserts.
    • Behavior: Compact, with shorter wings than others.
